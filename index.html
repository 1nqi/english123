About tsunami

Tsunamis are giant waves caused by earthquakes or volcanic eruptions under the sea. Out in the depths of the ocean, tsunami waves do not dramatically increase in height.

Tsunamis can

travel up rivers and streams that lead to the ocean. A large tsunami can flood low-lying coastal areas more than a mile inland.

Tsunamis generally reach

no more than 100 feet above sea level.

Interesting fact

Tsunami is a Japanese word that means 'Harbour Wave'.
