Causes of tsunami

Tsunamis are typically caused by large-scale disturbances in the ocean.

It can be underwater earthequakes, volcanic eruption, landslides, meteorite impacts, anthropogonecis causes.

It's important to note that tsunamis caused by underwater earthquakes are the most frequent and often the most devastating. When an undersea earthquake occurs, it can lead to the sudden uplift or subsidence of the seafloor, displacing an enormous amount of water and generating a series of powerful waves that propagate across the ocean. This displacement of water, coupled with the speed and energy of the waves, is what characterizes a tsunami and makes it so destructive when it reaches coastal areas.

Common causes

The most common causes of tsunami

Underwater Earthquakes

Submarine earthquakes are the most frequent cause of tsunamis.

Volcanic Eruptions

They particularly those that occur on or near the ocean floor, can displace large volumes of water and create tsunamis

Landslides

Landslides occurring underwater or in coastal areas can displace water and create tsunamis

Meteorite Impacts

Although extremely rare, the impact of a large meteorite in the ocean can create a tsunami by displacing a massive amount of water
